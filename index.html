Net neutrality

Would you like to pay extra fees for using common websites like facebook or youtube? Or maybe have someone watch over you at all times? We dont.

What is Net Neutrality?

The internet should be fair to use in forms of Net Neutraility is basically internet's guiding principle. It preserves our right to communicate freely online. For example: The internet as of now gives you the same download speed on every website you visit, you pay a set amount of money every month to get internet access and you dont have anyone monitoring every click you make on the world wide web (presumably).

And now, someone wants to take it away from us.

The First Problem

Recently, a big company in the US called the FCC, announced that they will basically just destroy the net neutrality laws.
Why is this a problem you ask?

This is problem because your internet service providers will be able to slow down websites of their choice.
Let me give you an example.

You are paying for your internet through the internet, one day a month. There's one day left until you have to pay, and you go to their website to pay. When you try to log in, it takes AGES to have the site load up. You have to go through multiple steps, which takes hours, and then, you miss your opportunity to pay. You get extra fees and so on.

This is not ok

So you decide to change your ISP, so you'll go look around on the internet. You find another website to a new ISP and try to open the website. "Oh whoops, this site has been blocked". What do you do?

This is ofcourse one of many examples on why this is bad.

The Second Problem

The second problem with no net neutrality is the your ISP can charge extra fees for you using a website.
