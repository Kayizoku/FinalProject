The Second Problem

The second problem with no net neutrality is that your ISP can charge extra fees for using certain websites or services of their choice.

So if you dislike that slow internet speed you suddenly got, you can just pay the cable companies extra fees. Great, right?

Paying for the internet might look more like paying for cable tv.
Having normal speeds for websites like Youtube or even apps like Snapchat might cost 30$ or more a month.
This, of course, is up to the cable companies if the FCC manages to remove net neutrality.
